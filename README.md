## Description
A performance overhaul for the **FS-20 Vortex**, transforming it from a capable multirole fighter into a true hypersonic superiority platform. This variant retrofits the airframe with heavy tech **originally engineered to open the way for Boscali spacecraft to punch through the dense layer of space debris above the Boscali atmosphere.** Every core system has been completely redesigned from the deck up to survive the brutal realities of exospheric clearing operations.
## Features
### ⚡ Quadruple Thrust VTOL
Engine thrust is boosted to **400,000 N** (4× stock), with VTOL thrusters scaled to match. Built to lift massive, debris-clearing payloads, the Vortex can now launch vertically from any airfield or carrier with **16 Tons Take-Off Weight**.
### 🔥 Scramjet Mode
Above **Mach 4.5 at 10,000 m**, the engine transitions into scramjet operation at **500,000 N**—the raw power curve required to swiftly reach intercept velocities. Engage and hold altitude to sustain hypersonic cruise. Disengage drops back to baseline thrust with hysteresis to prevent power flicker.
A pulsing **SCRAMJET ACTIVE** indicator appears on the HUD when the mode is live.
### 🌌 Operational Ceiling
Engine flames out at **50,000 m**, letting you graze the lower boundary of the debris fields. Below that, the altitude thrust curve is flat — ensuring zero power loss as you climb into the thin upper atmosphere.
### 📡 Sensor Suite
 * **Radar:** Detection range and signal strength doubled for sweeping wide sectors.
 * **EOTS:** Visual range extended to 50 km with 10× magnification. Originally calibrated to track erratic, hyper-velocity space debris moving up to 5,000 m/s, the tracking suite has been weaponized for absolute locking authority over combat targets.
### 🔋 Power System
Capacitor capacity, recharge rate, and max power output each increased **10×**. This massive grid overhaul ensures high-draw defense systems and scanning arrays stay online indefinitely.
### 🛡️ Airframe Hardening
 * Airbrake joints heavily reinforced for safe hypersonic deployment.
 * FBW pitch/roll damped above Mach 3 to eliminate the intense atmospheric oscillations encountered during high-altitude ascents.
 * Overspeed warnings suppressed up to **4,500 m/s** to unlock the true structural threshold required to clear a path to the void.
